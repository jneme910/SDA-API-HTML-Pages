Wisconsin Critical & Sensitive Area Assessment Report

A Area Risk Assessment under NRCS guidance is a process to identify and map ecologically or hydrologically sensitive land features so that conservation practices, grazing systems, or development can be planned to avoid or minimize harm. Select a Soil Survey Area (SSA). Sorted by Map Unit Name. Data from NRCS SDA Tabular API.

Definitions

  • Area Symbol — A symbol that uniquely identifies a single occurrence of a particular type of soil survey area (e.g., "WI025").
  • Area Name — The name given to a specific Soil Survey Area.
  • Map Unit Name — The correlated name of a map unit, which typically includes the names of the major soil components and often a slope range (e.g., "Antigo silt loam, 0 to 2 percent slopes").
  • MUKEY — A non-connotative string of characters used as a unique persistent identifier (primary key) for a record in the mapunit table.
  • MUYSM — The symbol used to uniquely identify a soil map unit within a specific soil survey (e.g., "C49A").
  • Slope Class Rating / localphase — localphase is a criterion used locally with the component name to identify specific soil components. Categorizes suitability based on slope_r: <12% is Suitable, 12–30% is 'Limited', and >=30% is Not Suitable.
  • Drainage Class Rating — Identifies natural drainage conditions and refers to the frequency and duration of wet periods. Categorizes suitability based on drainagecl: 'Well', 'Somewhat excessively', or 'Excessively drained' are Suitable; 'Somewhat poorly' or 'Moderately well' are 'Limited'; and 'Poorly' or 'Very poorly' are Not Suitable.
  • Flooding Class Rating — Identifies the annual probability of a flood event. Categorizes suitability based on flodfreq: 'None' is Suitable, 'Occasional' or 'Rare' is 'Limited', and 'Very Frequent' or 'Frequent' is Not Suitable.
  • Ponding Class Rating — Standing water in a closed depression that is removed only by percolation, transpiration, or evaporation. Categorizes suitability based on pondfreq: 'None' is Suitable, 'Occasional' or 'Rare' is 'Limited', and 'Very Frequent' or 'Frequent' is Not Suitable.
  • Restriction Kind / pondfreq — Describes the type of nearly continuous root-limiting soil restriction, such as "Lithic bedrock" or "Fragipan". Selects the first_restriction_kind for the dominant component to identify the primary physical limitation.
  • Restriction Depth Class Rating — The representative depth from the soil surface to the upper boundary of a restrictive layer. Categorizes suitability based on rv_first_restriction_depth: >=150cm is Suitable, 50–150cm is 'Limited', and <50cm is Not Suitable.
  • Hydric Class Rating — Proportion of the map unit that is hydric based on component flags. Derives a descriptive status ('Hydric', 'Nonhydric', 'Predominantly Hydric', 'Predominantly Nonhydric', or 'Partially Hydric') by comparing hydric component counts against the total map unit composition.
  • WI FSG ID — The Forage Suitability Group ID identifies soil components with similar potential and limitations for forage production. Aggregates ecological class IDs using dominant component logic, specifically filtered for Wisconsin ecological classes.
  • Not Suitable — This category applies to soils with one or more properties so unfavorable or difficult to overcome that the soil is generally considered unsatisfactory for the intended use [1]. The limitations (such as extreme slopes or very shallow restrictive layers) typically make the specified use impractical or cost-prohibitive.
  • Limited — This designation identifies soils that possess one or more properties that are moderately unfavorable for a specified use [1]. These constraints are significant enough to require special planning, custom design, or increased maintenance to ensure the soil performs as intended within the assessment criteria.
  • Suitable — This classification indicates that the soil has properties that are favorable or easily managed for a specific use [1]. Soils in this category generally do not require special conservation practices or design considerations to mitigate limitations for the intended use.
  • Not Rated — This is a neutral designation indicating that a soil component cannot be evaluated for a specific interpretation [3]. This typically occurs due to insufficient data in the database or because the soil component is not applicable to the specific land use criteria being assessed.
  • Null or No Data — This represents a state where no value exists in the database. It indicates that the underlying soil properties required to generate a rating or class were completely unpopulated in the source data, preventing any calculation or categorization from being attempted.
